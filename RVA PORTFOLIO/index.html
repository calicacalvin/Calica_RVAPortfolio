Artist Statement

This portfolio is a real look at my journey as an IT student exploring the world of visual arts. It’s not just about making things look "cool"—it’s about learning how to communicate ideas clearly, whether through a hand-drawn sketch or a digital poster. From the frustration of getting proportions right in Module 2 to the satisfaction of designing a clean infographic in Module 10, these works represent my effort to bridge the gap between creative expression and technical function.

Module 01

Visual World

An exploration of our digital landscape using collage.

About this work: I used icons and code snippets to visualize how deeply technology is integrated into our daily lives.
Module 02

7 Elements of Art

Mastering the basics through traditional sketching.

About this work: I focused on line and value to turn a flat piece of paper into a three-dimensional object.
Module 03

Abstract Design

Practicing design principles through abstract shapes.

About this work: I played with color and movement to see how shapes can lead the eye across the page.
Module 04

Transformation

A sequence showing an object evolving step-by-step.

About this work: I explored how one form can morph into another, showing that art is a process of constant change.
Module 05

Filipino-Inspired Pattern

Using photography and cultural symbols to create designs.

About this work: I integrated Baybayin and traditional motifs to showcase my heritage through design.
Module 06-07

Drawing/Painting

A deeper look at light, shadow, and color theory.

About this work: I focused on the "realness" of an object, working on how shadows give things weight.
Module 08

Module 09

Module 10

Infographic

Simplifying how the internet works through visual data.

About this work: This was a challenge in balancing text and graphics so that anyone can understand technical steps.
Module 11

About this work: I used memes to talk about the reality of student life and the struggle for a "Summer Break."
